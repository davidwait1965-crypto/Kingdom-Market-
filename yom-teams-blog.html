Youth on Mission: Where Faith Meets Action

YOM (Youth on Mission) isn’t just another youth group—it’s a movement of young believers who are done sitting on the sidelines. This is for the generation that wants to know God deeply and share His love boldly.

YOM Teams are young people who:

  • Live out their faith through real-world mission experiences
  • Share the Gospel with courage and conviction
  • Serve communities locally and globally
  • Grow as disciples through intentional training and mentorship
  • Step into their calling now instead of waiting for “someday”

Whether you’re 16 or 26, whether you have 2 weeks or 2 years—YOM has a place for you. This isn’t missions as usual. This is your generation rising up to advance God’s Kingdom on earth.

Not Just Talk. Real Mission. Real Impact.

YOM Teams are built on three core pillars that turn belief into bold, world-changing action.

Authentic Discipleship

YOM doesn’t just send you out—they equip you first. Through intensive training in prayer, the Word, and hearing God’s voice, you’ll be transformed from the inside out before you ever step foot on the mission field.

Bold Evangelism

No watered-down Gospel here. YOM Teams share Jesus unapologetically—on the streets, in villages, in schools, wherever God opens doors. You’ll learn to share your faith with confidence and watch God move powerfully.

Hands-On Service

Mission isn’t just preaching—it’s demonstrating God’s love through action. YOM Teams serve through mercy ministries, community projects, and meeting practical needs while sharing the hope of Jesus.

What Happens on a YOM Team?

Training Phase: Equipped to Go

Before you’re sent, you’re prepared.

YOM’s training isn’t just about learning facts—it’s about transformation. You’ll dive deep into:

  • Prayer & Intercession – partnering with God in prayer
  • Evangelism Training – sharing the Gospel with boldness
  • Discipleship – growing in your identity as a son or daughter of God
  • Leadership Development – stepping into the calling God has on your life
  • Spiritual Warfare – walking in the authority of Christ

Duration: Varies by program (typically 1–6 months of training and outreach combined)

Outreach Phase: Sent to Serve

This is where faith becomes action.

YOM Teams deploy to:

  • Urban centers – street evangelism, homeless ministry, campus outreach
  • Remote villages – church planting, community development, medical missions
  • International locations – cross-cultural ministry in nations hungry for the Gospel
  • Local communities – serving your own city with fresh eyes and bold faith

You’ll experience daily evangelism, see people encounter Jesus, and collect stories you’ll tell for the rest of your life.

Impact Phase: Forever Changed

You don’t just go on a mission trip—you become a missionary. YOM alumni describe their experience as life-altering.

Some continue into full-time ministry. Others return home as marketplace missionaries, bringing Kingdom impact to every sphere of society.

The question isn’t whether missions will change you. It’s whether you’re ready to be changed.

From Your Neighborhood to the Nations

YOM Teams go wherever God opens doors—and He’s opening them everywhere.

Recent YOM outreaches have included:

  • United States – inner-city evangelism, homeless outreach, campus ministry
  • Latin America – church planting, village ministry, youth camps
  • Africa – medical missions, orphan care, evangelism crusades
  • Asia – underground church support, creative access ministry
  • Europe – refugee ministry, street evangelism, revival gatherings

The mission field isn’t just “over there”—it’s everywhere. YOM Teams serve both locally and globally, because the Great Commission doesn’t have borders.

Is YOM Right for You?

YOM Teams are for young believers who are:

  • Hungry for more – tired of lukewarm Christianity and ready for radical faith
  • Willing to be stretched – you know growth happens outside your comfort zone
  • Passionate about Jesus – you want to know Him more and make Him known
  • Ready to serve – willing to put others first and serve sacrificially
  • Open to adventure – excited (and maybe a little nervous) about what God has for you
  • Committed to growth – ready to be discipled and become a disciple-maker

You don’t need to be “super spiritual” or have it all figured out. You just need to be willing to say YES to God.

Age Range: Typically 16–30 (varies by program)
Experience Required: None—just a heart for God and willingness to learn
Cost: Varies by trip (fundraising support and coaching available)

Ready to Step Into Your Calling?

Joining a YOM Team is simpler than you think. Here’s how to get started:

  • Step 1: Pray & Explore – Ask God if He’s calling you to missions. Visit YOMTeams.com to learn about upcoming trips, locations, and program options. Don’t overthink it—just take the first step.
  • Step 2: Choose Your Trip – Browse available YOM Teams based on: location, duration (2 weeks, 3 months, 1 year), focus (evangelism, mercy ministry, discipleship, church planting), and timing (summer, gap year, or semester break).
  • Step 3: Apply & Prepare – Complete your application, connect with YOM leadership, and begin preparing spiritually and practically. YOM will guide you through fundraising, training, and logistics.
  • Step 4: Go & Be Transformed – Step onto the mission field and watch God do more than you ever imagined—in the people you serve and in you.

Common Questions About YOM Teams

  • Q: Do I need mission experience to join?
    A: Nope! YOM provides all the training you need. Just bring a willing heart.
  • Q: How much does it cost?
    A: It varies by trip (typically $2,000–$5,000). YOM provides fundraising resources and coaching to help you get there.
  • Q: What if I’m still in school?
    A: Perfect! Many YOM trips are designed for summer breaks, gap years, or semester programs.
  • Q: Is it safe?
    A: YOM prioritizes safety with experienced leaders, risk management protocols, and 24/7 support.
  • Q: What if I don’t feel “called” to missions?
    A: Every believer is called to the Great Commission. The question is where and when—not if.
