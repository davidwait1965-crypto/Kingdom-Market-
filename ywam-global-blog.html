YWAM: A Global Family on Mission

A relaxed, story-style look at Youth With A Mission — the worldwide movement that’s helping ordinary people say a bold “yes” to God.

Worship · Missions · Community
By Kingdom Market · Celebrating ministries that carry the heartbeat of Jesus into the nations.

If you scroll through YWAM.org, you quickly realize this isn’t just an organization — it’s a giant, global family. Flags from every continent, stories from every corner of the world, and one simple focus: to know God and make Him known.

YWAM (Youth With A Mission) is a missions movement where teachers, baristas, artists, students, parents, and “ordinary” people step into the wild adventure of following Jesus wherever He leads.

What Makes YWAM Different?

YWAM is interdenominational and international. You’ll find people from many church backgrounds and nations all pulling in the same direction: spreading the love of Jesus through both words and action.

Big picture of the YWAM story:
  • Started in 1960 with a teenager’s vision of waves of young people sharing Jesus.
  • Now in thousands of locations worldwide, from mega-cities to tiny villages.
  • All ages welcome — the “youth” in YWAM is really about a youthful heart.

Life in a YWAM Community

A typical YWAM campus might look like this: morning worship with flags overhead, classes on God’s character, practical training, outreach prep, and late-night conversations that go way deeper than small talk.

Training + Going

Most people connect with YWAM through a Discipleship Training School (DTS): part Bible school, part missions training, part heart-deep reset. After several months of teaching, students head out on outreach to serve, pray, share Jesus, and love people in practical ways.

Why We Love Sharing YWAM on Kingdom Market

Kingdom Market exists to highlight what God is doing around the world and to point people toward communities that live with a Kingdom mindset. YWAM is exactly that: creative, brave, global, and centered on Jesus.

Whether you’re curious about missions or simply hungry for more of God, taking time to explore YWAM’s story might be a beautiful next step.
